Siddheshwar Temple [cite: 14]

Dedicated to Lord Siddheshwar (a form of Lord Shiva), this temple is a landmark located near the peaceful Siddheshwar Lake in the city center[cite: 15, 16]. Thousands of devotees visit daily, especially during the Siddheshwar Yatra in January[cite: 17].

Pandharpur [cite: 18]

Located about 70 km from Solapur, it is home to the Vitthal Rukmini Temple[cite: 19, 20]. It becomes a major hub during Ashadhi and Kartiki Ekadashi festivals during the Wari pilgrimage[cite: 21].

Akkalkot [cite: 22]

About 40 km from Solapur, this town is famous for the temple of Shri Swami Samarth Maharaj[cite: 23, 24]. The atmosphere is calm and peaceful, attracting visitors from across the country[cite: 26].

Solapur Fort (Bhuikot Fort) [cite: 27, 28]

Built during the Bahmani Sultanate period, this fort reflects medieval Indian military architecture and Solapur's rich historical heritage[cite: 29, 30].
